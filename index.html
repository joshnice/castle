These surveys are for all property owners with a house over the age of 30 years. They are not just for people with visual signs of issues. Over 70% of the surveys that are completed are dry and given the all clear which give the house owner peace of mind. The results of these surveys will be left verbally with the house owners and not passed onto any third parties. No personal information will be passed onto or sold to third parties.

If you have arranged a survey through one of our marketing team you will receive a phone call prior to your appointment time to confirm we are running to the allotted timetable. Please be advised that these surveys are very popular and sometimes re-arrangements may need to be made. We will also inform you of the name of the surveyor who will be calling. All our surveyors and marketing staff carry photo identification. If in any doubt please call 0800 345 70 83.

Services

Damp Proofing

Damp proofing is an overarching term that covers several different types of damp that can occur in a property and the way in which it is treated. We treat almost all known causes of damp within properties and repair the property to a pre decoration standard. All the works carried out are decided upon between the customer and the company before works begin and our trusted teams of fitters can answer any of the queries you may have on site. If you have concerns about your property and would value a second opinion, contact us now and we can arrange a visit.

Rising Damp

Rising damp is caused by the drawing of moisture from the ground into the walls of the property this water travels through the brickwork, via capillaries (small holes), like a network of tunnels soaking all the works in its path; meaning that any plasterworks or timberwork will also suffer from damp. The treatment involves a chemical injection into the motar course, also known as the damp proofing course or DPC, and in some instances a membrane is required to further protect the plasterworks. Depending on the age or the property there may or may not be a DPC already installed in the walls, however these can fail over time especially the older treatments and therefore the walls are no longer protected.

What to look for

Rising damp can be easier identified on the internal walls of a property by water marks on the wall that seem to resemble marks of the water level, such as markings of the tide on the side of a cliff. These water marks can be paired with salt markings that also travel through the water and cause damage to the plaster; which can be seen in the bubbling of paintwork, loosening of wallpaper or even the complete deterioration of the plasterworks themselves.

Penetrating Damp

This type of damp is the result of water ingress or infiltration through a hole created by a defect or age and degradation of the external wall to the property. If the brick or stone has gotten to an age where the degrading has forced it to become porous this would allow water in constantly at a certain point of the wall, this means that -unlike rising damp- the water isn't drawn up the wall but soaking through. This type of damp can lead to similar issues seen with rising damp such as damage to the structure of the wall and damage to the make-up of the internal property but can also lead to rotting of timbers due to the constant moisture entering the property.

What to look for

Similar to rising damp damage to the decorative aspects of the internal wall such as paint or paper falling off is not uncommon, however, the damage will not be at the base of the wall necessary as seen with rising damp; it could be anywhere along the wall or even ceiling. This follows with the issue of water or damp marks on the wall instead of showing a level of where the water has reached it may show a patch or spot where the water is penetrating the wall; in addition, the palter in this area may crumble or fall away from the wall itself.

Condensation Control Units

Condensation Control Units are the least invasive treatment we offer for problems that are being caused by the lifestyle or lack of ventilation within the property itself. It works to reduce the amount of damp and humidity and in turn reduce the risk of further issues regarding damp in the future.

What to look for

Black mould is always a good indication of condensation, it appears on the internal wall of the property due to high levels of moisture meeting with colder spots on the wall. In addition, high levels of condensation, after the use of a shower or bath, that take a long time to clear is also an indication that there are issues present regarding the ventilation of the property.

Dry Rot

In contrary to the name this type of rotting will mainly occur in damp timber, it is a fungal assault on the timber that causes severe structural damage to the wood and therefore the property as well. It causes the wood to go very brittle and can cause subsequent issues to the floor or structure that it was supporting. It can be caused by a dry rot spore which can grow given the right environment- such as oxygen, correct temperature and access to a food source, or via cross contamination from another source either whilst in state in the property or prior to installation. One of the main concerns of dry rot is that it can travel through inert materials and thus can cover a larger area.

What to look for

In order to find the rot, you must first expose the timber which could most likely be affected; this means that you will need to go into the sub floor or loft of the property in order to get a perspective of the whole problem. This can arise its own issues when properties do not have a cellar or loft, these issues are mitigated by the surveyors if an issue is suspected. One of the key signs of dry rot is fungal or mycelium growth in the timber which is said to resemble cotton wool, a white stringy substance. Another indication is smell, dry rot leaves a damp, musty smell within the property. Another tell tale sign of ry rot is the brick red spores that become prevalent as well as deep cuboidal cracking of the timber itself. If you think you have seen or smelt any issues do not hesitate to give our experienced team a call.

Wet Rot

This type of rot, similarly to dry rot, also has a fungal cause however it is an overarching term that can refer to different fungal species. Wet rot is easier to spot on the facings of the timbers as the process involves the fungus breaking down the wood and causing structural issues both within the timber and possibly further with the property. As the name suggests the main cause of this problem is wood that is in areas with high moisture which in turn causes damp or wet timber and thus attracts the fungus for the process to begin. This means that any issue with water ingress or leaks could lead to issues with wet rot down the line; this means that wet rot can be found where there are also issues with rising or penetrating damp and with condensation.

What to look for

As afore mentioned, if there has been an historical issue with leaks or water ingress to the property there is a higher chance of wet rot becoming an issue; thus keeping an eye on ventilation of the timbers and any structural changes to the floor in the area of the leak. Similarly, to dry rot, wet rot also causes the timbers to smell musty or damp. Another sign to look out for would be darkened spongy timbers, the timbers will look wet to look at and be crumbly or spongy to the touch. There cpuld also be fungal growth in the affected areas but solely on the timber. If you think that this could be an issue in your property, get in touch now.

Beetle Infestation

As the name suggests this is the process of beetles finding a home within the timbers of your house. Wood boring beetles leaving holes and eating their way through the timber can cause structural insecurities as well as cracking the wood or further damaging it. The beetles thrive in damp areas and can be a further problem in already in those damp and unventilated areas.

What to look for

Loss of structural integrity is always a key indicator when there is any diseased timber, this is no difference when there are beetles present; in fact even more so with the presence of beetles the wood will become very weak. Another red flag when looking for beetles within the timbers would be tiny circular holes along the wood where the beetles have eaten their way through, this is paired with another key sign which is wood dust or frass; this is the result of the beetles making their way through the wood and comes out as a brown/orange powder or dust.
